Abstract

Dating culture and habits have been irrevocably changed by the heavily technological world that we live in today, for better or for worse. With so many dating apps, websites, and tools, are there certain characteristics that statistically make one user stand out against another, or perhaps makes them more apt to find a relationship. We are seeking to assess whether previous findings regarding the predominant attributes that people look for while dating ‘in-person’ are the same as those that users look for on dating apps/websites such as Tinder, Bumble, and Hinge.

Visualization explanation

Welcome to our visualization!! After all of our hard work we managed to show you relevant information about a topic everyone is interested in: dating apps. Above we present relevant facts and information that could help you adapt or reject these platforms. For the creation of the first visualization we used the information provided in our survey which describes the main reason why users are active on their preferred dating app, and to provide a more insightful analysis we divided the results according to the age range of the user so we extract a relationship. Even though, as we can observe we have an uneven distribution regarding the ages of the users, we can extract relevant assumptions from the pies. We made the visualization interactive so we could present the information in a more fun and entertaining way to the user, facilitating the information retrieval. Our second visualization shows the number of meetups that our responders have done divided by ranges, from 0 meetups all the way to 20+ meetups. By meetups we mean the number of times that they have actually met with someone they started a conversation with inside a dating app. We have divided each column of match ranges into male and female matches to show the difference between our male and female users of dating apps. The third visualization breaks down the “vices” displayed on dating profiles. It breaks them into two categories: turn-ons and turn-offs. We have created this visualization to show the distribution of these traits among our population of users.

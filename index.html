Abstract

Dating culture and habits have been irrevocably changed by the heavily technological world that we live in today, for better or for worse. With so many dating apps, websites, and tools, are there certain characteristics that statistically make one user stand out against another, or perhaps makes them more apt to find a relationship. We are seeking to assess whether previous findings regarding the predominant attributes that people look for while dating ‘in-person’ are the same as those that users look for on dating apps/websites such as Tinder, Bumble, and Hinge.

Visualizations

Dating app usage w/ age ranges

The first visualization shows the relationship between the number 1 reason why people use dating apps and their corresponding age range. With this composition of pie charts that we have created we wanted to demonstrate how we can relate the reason behind the dating app usage and the age range that the users belong to.

Visualization explanation

For the creation of the visualization above we used the information provided in our survey which describes the main reason why users are active on their preferred dating app, and to provide a more insightful analysis we divided the results according to the age range of the user so we extract a relationship. Even though, as we can observe we have an uneven distribution regarding the ages of the users, we can extract relevant assumptions from the pies. For example, we can observe how the percentage of users between 24 and 26 increases when looking for a relationship out of the app.



Vices turn ons vs. turn offs

The second visualization breaks down the “vices” displayed on dating profiles. It breaks them into two categories: turn-ons and turn-offs. We have created this visualization to show the distribution of these traits among our population of users.
